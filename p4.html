Magic Friend mirrors the hand motions of player and plays item matching game with player. It gives good feedback when a social interaction is done, or when player chooses the right answer. It is:
  • Simple
    The simple and clear way of playing intensifies predictability for children.
  • Educational
    Repetitive matching training builds a sense of categorization.
  • Sensual
    Visual stimulation helps children stay focused on their targets.

Concepts

ABA (Applied Behaviour Analysis):
Children receive an abundance of positive feedback for demonstrating socially appropriate behaviors. This helps them to reinforce these positive social interactions.
Relation Building:
Children with autism have difficulty relating their own behaviors to environmental and social situations, and to others’ actions. Having a virtual friend mirroring his actions helps him to gain more sense of relation.

Functions

  • Mirrors the player’s hand motion
  • Hint possible social interactions with virtual friend such as hug or shake hands
  • Match items on the ground with suggested item in the air
  • Detects eye-contact and rewards it at all time
